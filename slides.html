NVivo is software for Qualitative Data Analysis


  • available for Mac and PC
  • free for UBC students, faculty, and staff
  • supported by the UBC Library Research Commons

NVivo helps you organize and analyze unstructured information, often text. While NVivo is not a replacement for reading articles, we can use it to:

  • Help prioritise reading or query papers
  • Get an overview of core concepts/ideas.
  • Analyse article metadata.

If purchasing separate license, costs more than $1000 USD. So if you're at all curious about the software now is the time to try it.

Why use NVivo?

In the lit review process you're looking for relationships, identifying themes, finding trends, patterns and gaps in the literature. A tool like NVivo is designed around the processes involved in doing this kind of work.

It has features that new access points into the materials you're looking at, making it possible to work across your sources all at once instead of just looking at them one at a time.

It gives you many ways of taking notes and recording your comments and notes as you're working, linking those notes so they're easier to find later

And it keeps everying in one place - your NVivo project is just one file, but that single file can contain your source material, your observations, any work you might to to code the text for themes - ideally in a way that's easier to keep track of.
